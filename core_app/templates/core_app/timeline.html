  1. 19/09/2016

    Applying to undergraduate degree programs

    During my secondary school studies in Hungary, I developed an interest in mathematics and physics and I regularly competed in the National Mathematics and Physics Competition (OKTV). Consequently, I decided to pursue a science undergraduate degree and I applied to electrical and electronic engineering degree programs at several universities in Hungary and the United Kingdom. These included the University of Manchester, the University of Southampton, the University of Surrey, the University of Birmingham, The University of Sheffield, and the Budapest University of Technology and Economics.

    I successfully got admitted to all of them, and I decided to go to the University of Manchester. There were two reasons why I wished to enroll there. Firstly, I wanted to be fluent in English to be able to acquire knowledge from international scientific literature in the future. Secondly, the program offered interesting courses at the intersection of hardware and software with an internationally outstanding quality of teaching and facilities.

  2. 07/06/2017

    First year at the University of Manchester

    At the beginning of my Bachelor’s studies, I was awarded the Success Entry Scholarship 2016 for "having achieved A*AA at A-Level or an equivalent score in entry qualification with the A* in Maths, Physics, Electronics or Further Maths." Over the first year, I mostly took mathematics and introductory electronics courses to build strong foundations for more involved modules. My general interest started shifting towards software rather than hardware. While doing my best in academia, I started exploring programming with Python through small projects and self-paced learning.

    I completed the first year with a cumulative GPA of 89 %, which ranked me within the top 5 % of my cohort.

  3. 20/07/2017

    Becoming interested in machine learning

    While studying the first-year modules at university and learning programming through self-paced learning, I developed an interest in machine learning. Therefore, I started Andrew Ng’s Machine Learning online course on Coursera and began reading books such as “An Introduction to Statistical Learning: With Applications in R”. Soon, I grew passionate about machine learning and decided to further explore this field of study in parallel to my Bachelor's studies.

  4. 18/09/2017

    Second year at the University of Manchester

    During the second year of my studies, my interest in machine learning led me to read a lot about deep learning and the need for efficient artificial intelligence accelerators. Therefore, I decided to specialize in electronic engineering so that I could gain a thorough understanding of computer systems architecture and electronic chip design. The modules I took helped me build strong foundations for understanding the need for efficient hardware for real-time artificial intelligence applications.

  5. 15/10/2017

    Team project to build an autonomous robot car

    I started working in a team of five to plan, design, and build a line following robot car that could autonomously navigate a designated track using a PID controller. As I took on the leading role in software development, I delegated programming tasks to other team members and mastered my skills in programming in C and C++ and using IoT software development platforms such as ARM Mbed.

  6. 16/07/2018

    A journey from the university to the industry

    I completed my second year at the University of Manchester with a cumulative GPA of 88 %, which helped me keep my position within the top 5 % of my cohort.

    Having finished two of the three years of the degree program, I decided to gain professional experience and put my theoretical knowledge to work in the context of a full-time, year-long internship with Nexperia, a Dutch semiconductor company.

  7. 03/09/2018

    First day as a full-time R&D intern

    My first day at Nexperia in Manchester in the United Kingdom as a full-time Research and Development Intern. I was tasked to build an internal scientific simulation and analysis software to optimize semiconductor device design and manufacturing.

    The software development was in Python, which I was already familiar with to a certain degree. However, this project was an opportunity to master my Python skills and develop software for industrial applications.

  8. 17/01/2019

    Presenting my project to the internal R&D forum

    Having been in development for four months, I gave my first presentation in the monthly, internal R&D company meeting that welcomed engineers from the United Kingdom, the Netherlands, Germany, and Singapore. In my 30-minute-long presentation, I described how the scientific simulation and analysis software optimized device design and manufacturing, and its value to the company in the long-term.

    My presentation induced interest and several other departments asked me after the presentation to integrate it into their workflow. Furthermore, I started working closely with the German branch to set up a full-scale, internal device design workflow that was going to be built in Python.

  9. 25/03/2019

    Making progress with the internship project

    By the half-time of the project, I had cleaned, explored, and visualized raw product manufacturing data, designed factorial experiments, and used statistical techniques such as ANOVA to extract actionable insights. I used these insights to develop the scientific simulation and analysis software in Python that had the potential to improve the company’s semiconductor device design process. Furthermore, I built interactive, easy-to-use GUIs with PyQt to improve accessibility and user experience.

  10. 16/04/2019

    Presenting to the internal R&D forum for the second time

    I delivered my second presentation in the monthly, internal R&D company meeting, during which I gave a live demonstration of the scientific simulation and analysis software. The meeting had several international attendees via video chat applications. In the questions and answer part of the meeting, I effectively responded to technical questions about the mathematical models and the underlying algorithms.

    A fellow engineer pointed out that deep learning could be integrated into the software, which would likely further improve the mathematical models. After the meeting, I spent days thinking about how to add deep learning to the software, but I realized that I did not have the necessary knowledge to do so. As I wanted to explore the viability of deep learning in this industrial application, I enrolled in the Deep Learning online course and specialization offered by deeplearning.ai on Coursera.

  11. 23/08/2019

    Handing over my internship project

    My last day as a Research and Development intern with Nexperia. I produced a 30-page-long documentation for the simulation and analysis software for the handover of the project. The completed software was fully-functional and it met all of the requirements. The GUI enabled user interaction with the software without CLI and the documentation listed all of the future areas of improvement to be implemented after the handover of the project.

    The list of technologies I used throughout my internship includes Python (NumPy, SciPy, Pandas, PyQt), Minitab, TIBCO Spotfire, Linux.

  12. 16/09/2019

    Bachelor's thesis project in deep learning-based object detection

    I returned from the full-time, year-long internship to the University of Manchester to resume my Bachelor’s studies. As my interest fully shifted towards deep learning by this time, I started my final year individual thesis project titled “Real-Time Object Detection on an Embedded GPU System” under the supervision of Dr. Hujun Yin.

    The goal of the project was to train deep learning-based object detection models in the NVIDIA Deep Learning GPU Training System (DIGITS), deploy the trained models for real-time object detection on an NVIDIA Jetson Nano embedded AI computing platform and adapt the platform to serve as the vision system of a Niryo One robotic arm.

  13. 01/12/2019

    Completing multiple online courses in deep learning

    I completed four of the five-course specialization titled “Deep Learning” offered by deeplearning.ai on Coursera. I learned to build and train deep neural networks and convolutional neural networks with Python and TensorFlow and applied them to visual detection and recognition tasks. I studied and applied techniques such as weight initialization, L2 and dropout regularization, Batch normalization, and gradient checking. Implemented a variety of optimization algorithms, such as mini-batch gradient descent, Momentum, RMSprop, and Adam.

    Course certificates:

    Neural Networks and Deep Learning,

    Improving Deep Neural Networks: Hyperparameter tuning, Regularization, and Optimization,

    Convolutional Neural Networks,

    Structuring Machine Learning Projects

  14. 03/04/2020

    Being admitted to the MSc Macine Learning program at KTH Stockholm

    While working on my final year individual thesis project, I grew certain that I wanted to pursue a career in machine learning and deep learning. Therefore, I applied to graduate degree programs offered by the KTH Royal Institute of Technology (Stockholm, Sweden), the University of Amsterdam (Amsterdam, Netherlands), the Technical University of Denmark (Copenhagen, Denmark), and the University College London (London, United Kingdom).

    Upon being successfully admitted to all of the programs, I decided to accept my offer for the MSc Machine Learning at the KTH Royal Institute of Technology. The reason I chose KTH is that the diverse palette of courses offered in the program range from probability, statistics, and machine learning to the latest application of deep learning in computer vision and speech recognition as well as in reinforcement learning.

  15. 01/05/2020

    Submitting the Bachelor's thesis project report

    After seven months of hard work and studying, I submitted my final year individual thesis project report in partial fulfillment of the requirements for the BEng Electronic Engineering degree. The main project activities were as follow:

    Building, pre-processing, labeling, and cleaning an object detection dataset of 1,600 images

    Training deep learning-based object detection models in the NVIDIA Deep Learning GPU Training System (DIGITS)

    Conducting hyperparameter tuning to improve model performance

    Evaluating the trained models using the PASCAL VOC scheme

    Deploying the trained models for real-time object detection on an NVIDIA Jetson Nano embedded AI computing platform

    Adapting the platform to serve as the vision system of a Niryo One robotic arm that functioned as a pick-and-place machine

    Presenting my work in a 12,000-word report and a 15-minute-long presentation

    Deploying the trained models for real-time object detection on an NVIDIA Jetson Nano embedded AI computing platform

    Using tools and technologies including Caffe, Python, NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, scikit-learn, TensorFlow, OpenCV, NVIDIA DIGITS, Linux
